THE GOA PANCHAYAT RAJ ACT, 1994  
(Goa Act No. 14 of 1994)  
[Received the assent of the Governor of Goa on 9th July 1994; published in the Official Gazette on 13th July 1994]

AN ACT to replace the present enactment relating to Panchayats by a comprehensive legislation to establish a two-tier Panchayat Raj System in the State, with elected bodies at village and district levels, in keeping with the Constitution (Seventy-Third Amendment) Act, for greater participation of the people and more effective implementation of rural development programmes. Be it enacted by the Legislative Assembly of the State of Goa in the Forty-fifth Year of the Republic of India as follows: :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

CHAPTER I  
PRELIMINARY

1. Short title and commencement. —  
(1) This Act may be called the Goa Panchayat Raj Act, 1994.  
(2) Section 245 shall be deemed to have come into force with effect from 20th April 1994, while the remaining sections shall come into force on such date as the Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint; different dates may be appointed for different sections or provisions of the Act. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

2. Definitions. — In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires:  
(1) “Backward classes” means such classes of citizens as may be notified by the Government from time to time as belonging to backward classes;  
(1‑A) “Block Development Officer” means the person appointed as such by the Government;  
(1‑B) “Ballot” means ballot papers prepared in accordance with the provisions of the Act or rules made thereunder and includes “Electronic Voting Machine”;  
(2) “building” includes a house, outhouse, stable, privy, urinals, shed, hut, wall, and any other structure whether of masonry, bricks, wood, metal or any other material, but does not include a temporary structure erected on ceremonial or festive occasion or a tent;  
(3) “Sarpanch” means the Sarpanch of a Panchayat;  
(4) “Deputy Sarpanch” means the Deputy Sarpanch of a Panchayat;  
(5) “Adhyaksha” means the Adhyaksha of a Zilla Panchayat;  
(6) “Upadhyaksha” means the Upadhyaksha of a Zilla Panchayat;  
(7) “Collector” means the Collector of the district and includes an Additional Collector;  
(8) “Chief Executive Officer” means the Chief Executive Officer of a Zilla Panchayat and includes any other officer appointed by the Government in this behalf;  
(8‑A) “Deputy Director” means the person appointed as the Deputy Director of Panchayats by the Government;  
(9) “Director” means the person appointed as the Director of Panchayats under this Act and includes an Additional Director;  
(10) “district” means a revenue district;  
(11) “Government” means the Government of Goa;  
(11‑A) “Gram Sevak” means the person appointed by the Director to perform the duties as Gram Sevak;  
(12) “land” includes land which is built upon or covered with water;  
(12‑A) “Member‑Secretary” means a person appointed by the Government as Member‑Secretary and includes a Block Development Officer;  
(13) “notification” means a notification published in the Official Gazette;  
(14) “Panchayat” means a Village Panchayat established under section 3;  
(15) “erection or re‑erection or enlargement” of any building includes––  
 (i) any material alteration or enlargement of any building;  
 (ii) the conversion, by structural alteration, into a place of human habitation of any building not originally meant or constructed for human habitation;  
 (iii) conversion of one or more places of human habitation into a greater or lesser number;  
 (iv) addition of rooms or other structures;  
 (v) structural changes affecting sanitation or security;  
 (vi) conversions between use types (e.g., shop, religious building) or roofing of open spaces;  
(16) “factory” means, besides a factory as defined in the Factories Act, 1948, any premises where industrial manufacturing or trade is carried on with mechanical power;  
(16‑A) “political party” means as defined under clause (f) of sub‑section (1) of section 2 of the Representation of the People Act, 1951;  
(17) “prescribed” means prescribed by rules under this Act;  
(18) “Schedule” means a Schedule appended to this Act;  
(19) “Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes” means such as specified for Goa under the Constitution;  
(20) “Secretary” means the Secretary of the Panchayat;  
(20‑A) “Taluka Panchayat” means a Taluka Panchayat established under this Act;  
(21) “Zilla Panchayat” means a Zilla Panchayat established under this Act. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}


CHAPTER II  
GRAM SABHA AND PANCHAYATS

3. Declaration of Panchayat areas and establishment of Panchayats. —  
(1) After making such inquiry as may be necessary, the Government may, by notification, declare a local area, comprising a village or a group of villages or part thereof, to be a Panchayat area for the purposes of this Act and specify its headquarters.  
(2) For every Panchayat area, there shall be a Panchayat from such date as the Government may, by notification, appoint. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}


CHAPTER III  
ELECTION OF PANCHAYATS

4. Election authority; reservation; term of office; etc. —  
(1) The State Election Commission shall be the election authority for conducting elections under this Act.  
(2) Elections to the Panchayats shall be held in such manner and at such time as the Election Commission may, in consultation with the Government, determine.  
(3) Seats reserved for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, women, and backward classes shall be allotted in each Panchayat in the prescribed manner.  
(4) Every Panchayat shall continue for a term of five years from the date of its first meeting unless sooner dissolved.  
(5) No person shall continue as a member of a Panchayat after the expiry of its term, but until the result of the succeeding election is declared.  
(6) Subject to the provisions of this Act and the rules made thereunder, a Panchayat shall, notwithstanding its dissolution, act as an ad hoc Panchayat in respect of all matters except elections, until the constitution of a new Panchayat.

5. Reservation of seats in Panchayats. —  
(1) Seats in every Panchayat shall be reserved for members of Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes in proportion to their population in the Panchayat area.  
(2) Not less than one-third of the total seats shall be reserved for women; among these, reservation shall be made for women belonging to Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, and other categories, in such rotation as may be prescribed.  
(3) Seats shall also be reserved for backward classes as prescribed.  
(4) The offices of Sarpanch and Deputy Sarpanch shall be reserved for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, and women in such proportion as the rules specify.

6. Qualifications for membership. —  
(1) A person shall be qualified to be a member of a Panchayat if they:  
  (a) are a citizen of India;  
  (b) are not less than twenty-one years of age;  
  (c) are a registered voter in the Panchayat area; and  
  (d) are not disqualified under this Act or any other law.  
(2) A person ceases to hold office if they:  
  (a) resign in writing;  
  (b) are adjudged insolvent;  
  (c) are convicted of an offence involving moral turpitude;  
  (d) are otherwise disqualified by the Act or rules;  
  (e) absence from three consecutive meetings without permission.

7. Corrupt practices. —  
(1) No candidate or member shall engage in corrupt practices such as bribery, undue influence, impersonation, appeals to religion or caste, or publication of false statements, as defined by the Representation of the People Act, 1951.  
(2) Any person found guilty shall be punishable as prescribed, and his election may be declared void by the Election Commission.

8. Election petitions. —  
(1) Any election to a Panchayat may be challenged by petition before the State Election Commission.  
(2) Such petitions shall be filed within thirty days of the declaration of results, in the prescribed format.  
(3) The Commission shall decide every petition within six months and may annul the election if valid grounds are established, ordering a fresh poll where necessary.

9. Disqualification on defection, dissolution of office. —  
(1) A member of a Panchayat is disqualified for being a member of another Panchayat, municipality, or parliament/state legislature.  
(2) A member ceases to hold office if the Panchayat under which they were elected is dissolved.

10. Filling of casual vacancies. —  
(1) A casual vacancy (due to death, resignation, disqualification, or otherwise) shall be filled by election within six months.  
(2) No election is required if the vacancy occurs less than six months before the Panchayat's term expires.

11. Electoral rolls. —  
(1) The electoral roll for a Panchayat area shall be the relevant part of the electoral roll maintained by the Election Commission of India.  
(2) The State Election Commission shall publish and update these rolls before every election.

12. By‑elections. —  
(1) If a vacancy exists due to annulment or court order, a by‑election shall be held within six months.  
(2) If no by‑election is held and the Panchayat’s term expires, the vacancy remains till term end.

13. Election expenses and returns. —  
(1) Every candidate shall adhere to the expenditure limit prescribed.  
(2) Candidates shall file expenditure returns within thirty days of election.  
(3) Failure to comply constitutes a corrupt practice and may attract penalties.

14. Nomination of candidates for Sarpanch, Deputy Sarpanch. —  
(1) Elections for Sarpanch and Deputy Sarpanch shall be held in the prescribed manner.  
(2) The election schedule, nomination form, and election process shall follow the rules.

15. Procedure for the nomination meeting. —  
Rules shall prescribe the procedure for convening and conducting nomination meetings by the Election Commission.

16. Publication of list of members. —  
As soon as results are declared, the Director of Panchayats shall publish the names of all elected members including Sarpanch, Deputy Sarpanch, and ward members in the Official Gazette and Panchayat notice board, within seven days.

17. Oath of office. —  
Every elected member shall take an oath or affirmation of allegiance and commitment to duties in such form as prescribed, before entering upon office.


CHAPTER IV  
ZILLA PANCHAYAT

18. Constitution of Zilla Panchayat. —  
(1) For every district there shall be a Zilla Panchayat consisting of:  
   (a) one member elected by and from among the elected members of the Panchayats within the district;  
   (b) the members of Parliament and the Legislative Assembly whose constituencies lie wholly or partly within the district;  
   (c) the Adhyaksha and Upadhyaksha of the Zilla Panchayat elected as provided in section 20;  
   (d) the Block Development Officers in the district as ex-officio Members‑Secretary.  

19. Term of Zilla Panchayat. —  
(1) The Zilla Panchayat shall continue for five years from its first meeting unless sooner dissolved.  
(2) It shall exercise its functions and perform duties even after dissolution, until the new body is constituted, except for election purposes.  

20. Election of Adhyaksha and Upadhyaksha. —  
(1) At the first meeting, the Zilla Panchayat shall elect its Adhyaksha and Upadhyaksha from among its members.  
(2) An officer specified by the Government shall preside over the meeting in which the election is held but shall not vote.  
(3) Reservations apply to these positions as prescribed.  

21. Casual vacancies. —  
(1) Any casual vacancy in the office of Adhyaksha or Upadhyaksha shall be filled by election within six months in the same manner as original elections.  
(2) No election is required if the vacancy occurs within six months before the end of the term.

22. Disqualifications and removal. —  
(1) A member shall cease to hold office on becoming disqualified under section 10.  
(2) A motion of no-confidence may be moved against the Adhyaksha or Upadhyaksha. A majority vote at a special meeting will remove them.  

23. Meetings and procedures. —  
(1) The Zilla Panchayat shall meet at least once every three months at the place and time fixed by the Adhyaksha.  
(2) Special meetings may be called by the Adhyaksha or on requisition by one-third of members.  
(3) Seven days’ notice is required for special meetings unless waived.  
(4) Quorum for a meeting is one-third of total membership; if quorum is not present within 15 minutes, the meeting stands adjourned until the same day/time next week.  
(5) Voting, unless a division is demanded, shall be by voice.

24. Powers and functions. —  
The Zilla Panchayat shall have all powers and perform functions prescribed under this Act and rules, which include but are not limited to:  
   (a) preparation of development plans for the district;  
   (b) technical supervision of schemes and budgets of Taluka and Village Panchayats;  
   (c) coordination within the district and reporting progress to the Government.

25. Committees. —  
The Zilla Panchayat shall constitute standing and other committees in the prescribed manner.

26. Zilla Panchayat Fund. —  
(1) There shall be constituted a Zilla Panchayat Fund:  
   (a) grants from government, central/state;  
   (b) loans;  
   (c) taxes/fees charged by the Panchayat;  
   (d) rents from Zilla Panchayat properties;  
   (e) any other contributions or income. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}  
(2) Withdrawals from the Fund shall be as per the budget sanctioned and subject to Financial Commission restrictions.

27. Budget and accounts. —  
(1) Each year, before a government-determined date, the Zilla Panchayat must submit its budget to the Government.  
(2) The Government may modify the budget within two months to ensure compliance with the Act and rules.  
(3) Accounts and audit procedures shall be maintained under the prescribed rules.

28. Audit and inspection. —  
(1) The Government may appoint auditors to audit Zilla Panchayat funds, requiring certified financial statements.  
(2) The Chief Executive Officer shall prepare an administrative report annually for the year ending March 31 and submit it to the Zilla Panchayat and Government.  
(3) Zilla Panchayat decisions may be inspected for legality and propriety by prescribed authorities.

29. Reports and returns. —  
The Zilla Panchayat shall submit reports/returns to the Government at intervals in prescribed formats.

30. Dissolution. —  
(1) The Government may dissolve the Zilla Panchayat if its body cannot function or is not in public interest.  
(2) On dissolution:  
   (a) members vacate office;  
   (b) the Government may nominate interim members;  
   (c) a new body must be constituted within six months; and  
   (d) assets, liabilities, and rights shall transfer to the new body.

(1) The Zilla Panchayat shall constitute the following standing committees in the prescribed manner:  
   (a) General Standing Committee;  
   (b) Finance, Audit and Planning Committee;  
   (c) Social Justice Committee;  
   (d) Education & Health Committee;  
   (e) Agriculture & Industries Committee;  
   (f) Taluka Development Committee.

(2) Each committee shall consist of not more than five members elected by the Zilla Panchayat from among themselves. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

(4) The Upadhyaksha serves ex‑officio as Chairman of the Social Justice Committee.  
(5) Chairmen of other committees are elected by their respective members.

Functions of these committees include reviewing proposals, financial oversight, audit, planning, social justice implementation, health and education monitoring, and agricultural/industrial development. 33. Taluka Panchayat Administration. —  
Taluka Panchayats shall also form similar standing committees with Chairs and Secretaries in accordance with prescribed rules and regulations. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}  

34. Director and Deputy Director. —  
(1) The Government shall appoint a Director of Panchayats and a Deputy Director to oversee implementation of this Act statewide.  
(2) Duties include coordination between various Panchayats, advising on administration, enforcing rules, conducting training, and serving as appellate authority. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}  

35. Chief Executive Officer and Secretaries. —  
(1) A Chief Executive Officer shall be appointed for each Zilla Panchayat, responsible for administration including planning, financial management, implementation, and reporting.  
(2) Village Panchayat Secretaries (Gram Sevaks) and Taluka Panchayat Secretaries assist in management at the grassroots and block levels. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Terms of service, appointments, transfers, and disciplinary procedures for Panchayat officers (including SEC) shall be governed by rules prescribed by the Government. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}  


CHAPTER VI  
POWERS AND FUNCTIONS

37. Powers of Collector to prohibit fairs etc. on election day. —  
The Collector may, in order to ensure free and fair elections, prohibit any fair (including cattle fairs) in a Panchayat area on the day of an election to that Panchayat. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

38. Functions of Gram Sabha. —  
(1) The Sarpanch shall place before the Gram Sabha for its approval:  
   (a) the annual statement of accounts;  
   (b) the annual administration report;  
   (c) the budget estimates;  
   (d) development programmes for the current fiscal year;  
   (e) the last audit report and responses thereto;  
   (f) proposals for fresh or increased taxation;  
   (g) community service or voluntary labour initiatives;  
   (h) identification of beneficiaries under government schemes;  
   (i) setting priorities for Panchayat works;  
   (j) utilisation certificates for completed work funded by grants or Panchayat funds.  
(2) At least two supervisory committees shall be constituted to oversee Panchayat activities.  
(3) The Government shall constitute Vigilance Committees for each Gram Panchayat.  
(4) Decisions of the Gram Sabha are binding on the Panchayat unless they contravene rules or law, and the Sarpanch must act on them promptly. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

39. General powers of the Panchayat. —  
(1) Panchayats may perform all acts necessary or incidental to carry out functions entrusted, assigned, or delegated under this Act—particularly, those specified in Schedule I.  
(2) Functions include, but are not limited to, maintaining roads, bridges, drains, wells, and public places in Panchayat areas—with powers to repair, improve, and raise objections before altering infrastructure—and subject to Zilla Panchayat approval where applicable. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

   - Promote agriculture, animal husbandry, rural industries, and poverty alleviation via standing committees. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}  
   - Implement social justice initiatives, welfare for SC/ST, women, children through dedicated committees. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}  
   - Address amenities like education, public health, and works via elected Amenities Committees. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}  
   - Vest/purchase land and properties for Panchayat purposes, and lease or manage Panchayat assets, as prescribed.  

41. Powers of Taluka and Zilla Panchayats. —  
Taluka and Zilla Panchayats possess similar general powers to undertake health, safety, educational, welfare, economic, or cultural work within their jurisdiction, form associations with higher bodies, supervise fund usage, call for reports from subordinate Panchayats, and, in the case of Zilla Panchayats, regulate unwieldy traffic as prescribed. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}


CHAPTER VII  
FUNDS AND FINANCE

161. Constitution of Panchayat Fund —  
(1) Every Panchayat shall have a fund called the Panchayat Fund.  
(2) The Fund shall consist of—  
   (a) grants and loans from the Government;  
   (b) taxes, rates, rents, fees, penalties, and other charges levied by the Panchayat;  
   (c) donations, subscriptions or contributions;  
   (d) receipts from transfer of rights and other income;  
   (e) sums recovered by or on behalf of the Panchayat;  
   (f) income from investments made under this Act.

162. Custody, withdrawal and investment —  
(1) All sums credited to the Panchayat Fund shall be kept in the Government Treasury or public account.  
(2) The Panchayat, with Government sanction, may deposit surplus funds in a Scheduled or Co‑operative bank, or invest in Government securities.  
(3) Withdrawals require signatures of the Sarpanch and Secretary, against expenditures budgeted and sanctioned.

163. Budget estimates —  
(1) Each Panchayat shall prepare estimates of its income and expenditure in the prescribed form by a Government-specified date.  
(2) The estimates shall be placed before the Gram Sabha for discussion and approval.
164. Sanction of budget proposals —  
(1) The Panchayat, after Gram Sabha approval, shall forward the budget to the Director for sanction or modification.  
(2) The Director must finalize or return the budget to the Panchayat within two months, communicating any modifications.

165. Panchayat accounts —  
(1) Every Panchayat shall maintain accounts in prescribed forms.  
(2) These accounts shall be audited annually by an authority appointed by the Government.

166. Application of Panchayat Fund —  
Withdrawals from the Fund shall be applied for purposes authorized in the budget estimates as approved and sanctioned.

167. Custody and investment of Zilla Panchayat Fund —  
(1) Funds shall be kept in the Government Treasury.  
(2) With Government approval, surplus funds may be deposited in banks or invested in Government securities.  
(3) The government may authorize variation or disposal of such investments. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

168. Zilla Panchayat Fund —  
(1) Every Zilla Panchayat shall maintain its own Fund consisting of—  
   (a) grants and loans received;  
   (b) taxes, fees or charges;  
   (c) rents from property;  
   (d) income from investments;  
   (e) contributions, receipts, or other income.  
(2) Funds shall be spent only with proper budget authorization.

169. Budget estimates of Zilla Panchayat —  
(1) Zilla Panchayat shall prepare its budget in prescribed format before a Government‑specified date.  
(2) Forwarded to Government for sanction and may be modified within two months.

170. Accounts and audit —  
(1) Annual accounts to be maintained in prescribed forms.  
(2) Government-appointed auditors shall audit and have the power to inspect.  
(3) CEO must submit an administrative report each year.

171. Recovery of money due —  
Any money due to a Panchayat may be recovered as an arrear of land revenue.

172. Write-off of irrecoverable amounts —  
(1) The Director may write off such irrecoverable amounts as prescribed.  
(2) Limits may be set under rules.


CHAPTER VIII  
AUDIT AND INSPECTION

173. Audit of accounts. —  
(1) The Government shall appoint an auditor to audit the accounts of each Panchayat, Taluka Panchayat, and Zilla Panchayat.  
(2) The auditor shall complete the audit within six months from the end of each financial year, and submit the report to the Director of Panchayats.  
(3) The Director of Accounts shall send certified copies of the audit report to the concerned Panchayat, along with information on any action taken. ([turn0search12](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/152449762/))

174. Technical supervision and inspections. —  
(1) Heads of Departments concerned and Divisional-level officers may inspect works or developmental schemes under the control of any Panchayat, Zilla Panchayat, or Taluka Panchayat and relevant documents, in the manner specified by the Government.  
(2) Such inspections may cover technical aspects including feasibility, economic viability, work quality, and expenditure.  
(3) Inspection reports shall be sent to the Chief Executive Officer or Secretary for further action. ([turn0search3](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/150138917/))

175. Powers of entry for inspection. —  
(1) Any officer or employee appointed by a Panchayat may, between sunrise and sunset, enter any house, building, land, drain, sewer, privy, water-closet, house-gully, or cesspool to inspect or execute works for purposes under this Act.  
(2) Reasonable notice must be given to occupants or owners, unless urgent public health measures require immediate action.  
(3) Costs of repair, reinstatement, or remedial work due to inspection may be charged to the owner—unless defects arose due to illegality or contravention of by-laws, in which case they bear the cost. ([turn0search8](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/37077714/), [turn0search15](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/19636132/))

176. Submission of audit rectifications. —  
(1) A Panchayat must submit its rectification report within one month of receiving audit feedback.  
(2) On receipt, the Chief Executive Officer may accept the explanation, withdraw objections, request reinvestigation, or direct remedial action in consultation with the auditor. ([turn0search16](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/124718609/))

177. Action on audit and inspection. —  
(1) The Director shall forward a summary of audit observations and Panchayat rectifications to the Government, which shall lay it in the Legislative Assembly. ([turn0search12](https://indiankanoon.org/doc/152449762/))  
(2) Non-compliance or failure to act upon audit or inspection findings may result in a Government order directing corrective action—with possible suspension of funds or administrative control until resolved.
